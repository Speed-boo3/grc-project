# Information Security Policy

**Version:** 1.0
**Last updated:** March 2024
**Owner:** Security Team

---

## Purpose

This policy sets out the basic rules for keeping systems and data secure. It applies to everyone who uses company systems — employees, contractors and anyone working in a lab environment.

---

## Access Control

Accounts should only have access to what they need. Admin access should be given out sparingly and reviewed every three months. MFA is required for all remote access.

Accounts unused for 90 days should be disabled. Passwords must be at least 12 characters and not reused across accounts.

---

## Logging and Monitoring

All servers and network devices should send logs to a central location. Logs must be kept for at least 90 days.

Alerts rated high or above must be investigated within 30 minutes. The SOC team reviews alerts daily.

---

## Patch Management

Critical patches must be applied within 7 days of release. All other patches within 30 days. Patch status is tracked and reported monthly.

---

## Incident Response

All security incidents must be reported to the SOC team immediately. The team follows the incident response playbook. A post-incident report is required for all high and critical incidents within 5 business days.

---

## Data Handling

Sensitive data must be encrypted at rest and in transit. It must not be stored on personal devices. Data that is no longer needed is securely deleted following the retention schedule.

---

## Physical Security

Workstations must be locked when unattended. Clean desk policy applies — no sensitive documents left on desks overnight. Visitors must be escorted in secure areas.

---

## Exceptions

Any exception to this policy must be documented and approved by the security team. Exceptions are reviewed every 6 months.

---

## Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action. The security team has the right to monitor systems for compliance.
