# Resources for GRC Students

A curated list of free, high-quality resources for learning GRC. Everything here is freely available online.

---

## Essential reading

**NIST SP 800-30 Rev 1 — Guide for Conducting Risk Assessments**
The definitive guide on how to assess risk systematically. Required reading for anyone working in GRC.
https://csrc.nist.gov/publications/detail/sp/800-30/rev-1/final

**NIST SP 800-37 Rev 2 — Risk Management Framework**
Explains the seven-step RMF process in detail. Heavily used in government and defence contexts.
https://csrc.nist.gov/publications/detail/sp/800-37/rev-2/final

**NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5 — Security and Privacy Controls**
The full catalogue of security controls. Over 1000 pages. You do not need to read it cover to cover, but you need to know it exists and how to navigate it.
https://csrc.nist.gov/publications/detail/sp/800-53/rev-5/final

**NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0**
The updated framework with the new Govern function. Free PDF download from NIST.
https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/CSWP/NIST.CSWP.29.pdf

**ENISA — How to set up a CSIRT and SOC**
Practical guidance from the EU Agency for Cybersecurity on building security operations.
https://www.enisa.europa.eu/publications/how-to-set-up-csirt-and-soc

## Frameworks overview

**ISO 27001 overview**
https://www.iso.org/isoiec-27001-information-security.html

**CIS Controls**
20 prioritised security controls. Very practical and good for smaller organisations.
https://www.cisecurity.org/controls

**COBIT**
IT governance framework from ISACA. Useful for understanding how GRC connects to business objectives.
https://www.isaca.org/resources/cobit

**GDPR text**
The full text of the General Data Protection Regulation.
https://gdpr-info.eu/

**OWASP Top 10**
The ten most critical web application security risks. Important context for GRC work involving web applications.
https://owasp.org/www-project-top-ten/

Free online courses and videos on NIST frameworks.

**CISA Free Training**
Free cybersecurity training from the US Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency.
https://www.cisa.gov/cybersecurity-training-exercises

**Open Security Training**
Free university-level security courses.
https://opensecuritytraining.info/

## Certifications worth knowing about

**CISA** (Certified Information Systems Auditor) from ISACA. One of the most respected GRC certifications. Good for audit and compliance roles.

**CRISC** (Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control) from ISACA. Focused on risk management.

**ISO 27001 Lead Implementer** or **Lead Auditor**. Practical certifications for ISO 27001 work.

**CompTIA Security+** is a good entry point for general security knowledge before specialising in GRC.

## Books (free access)

**NIST Interagency Reports (NISTIRs)**
Various shorter guides on specific security topics. All free.
https://csrc.nist.gov/publications/nistirs

**OWASP Testing Guide**
Free, comprehensive guide to security testing. Relevant for GRC analysts assessing application security controls.
https://owasp.org/www-project-web-security-testing-guide/
