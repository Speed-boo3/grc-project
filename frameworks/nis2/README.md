# NIS2 Directive (2024)

## What it is

The Network and Information Security 2 Directive (NIS2) is EU legislation that came into force across member states in October 2024. It significantly expands the scope and requirements of the original NIS Directive from 2016.

If your organisation operates in the EU or provides services to EU customers, NIS2 may apply to you.

---

## Who it applies to

NIS2 covers two categories:

**Essential entities** — energy, transport, banking, financial market infrastructure, health, drinking water, wastewater, digital infrastructure, ICT service management, public administration, space.

**Important entities** — postal and courier services, waste management, chemicals, food production, manufacturing, digital providers (search engines, online marketplaces, social networks), research.

Organisations with 50+ employees or €10M+ turnover in these sectors are likely in scope.

---

## Key requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| **Incident reporting** | Early warning within 24 hours. Full notification within 72 hours. Final report within 1 month. |
| **Risk management** | Documented cybersecurity risk management measures must be in place |
| **Supply chain security** | Security of suppliers and service providers must be assessed |
| **Board accountability** | Senior management personally liable for compliance failures |
| **Business continuity** | Backup management, disaster recovery and crisis management plans required |
| **Encryption** | Use of encryption and multi-factor authentication required |
| **Vulnerability handling** | Coordinated vulnerability disclosure policy required |
