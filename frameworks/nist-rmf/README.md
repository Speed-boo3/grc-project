# NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)

## What is it

The NIST RMF is a structured process for integrating security and privacy into systems throughout their lifecycle. It is widely used in US federal government and defence, but is relevant for any organisation that takes a systematic approach to risk.

## The seven steps

**Prepare** - Set up the organisational context. Define roles, identify common controls, establish risk tolerance.

**Categorise** - Classify the system based on the impact of a potential breach. Low, Moderate or High.

**Select** - Choose the security controls from NIST SP 800-53 that match the category.

**Implement** - Put the controls in place and document how they are implemented.

**Assess** - Check that the controls are working as intended.

**Authorise** - A senior official reviews the risk and formally accepts or rejects it.

**Monitor** - Continuously track the controls and the risk environment over time.

## Relationship to other frameworks

NIST RMF is more detailed and process-oriented than NIST CSF. CSF tells you what to do. RMF tells you exactly how to do it, step by step. They are designed to work together.

NIST SP 800-53 is the catalogue of security controls that RMF draws from. It has hundreds of controls across 20 families.
